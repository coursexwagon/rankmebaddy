## Pre-writing: Semantic Field Extraction

Before writing a single word, extract the full semantic field for the target keyword.

**Step 1 — SERP Extraction**
Search the target keyword and record:
- Every People Also Ask question (expand all of them)
- Every Related Search at the bottom
- All H2/H3 subheadings visible in the featured snippet (if one exists)
- The format of the current featured snippet (paragraph / list / table)

**Step 2 — Competitor Content Map**
Open the top 5 ranking pages. Record:
- Every H2 and H3 heading
- Topics covered that others miss
- Topics ALL of them cover (these are non-negotiable inclusions)
- Any original data or research they cite

**Step 3 — Semantic Field Assembly**
Combine all PAA questions + related searches + competitor headings.
Remove duplicates. Cluster by subtopic.
This cluster map IS your article outline.

## Content Templates by Intent

### Informational Content (Guides, Explainers)
```
Structure:
[Title] — include primary keyword + power word + year
[Meta description] — benefit-forward, 150–160 chars, includes secondary keyword

[Introduction — 100–150 words]
- Sentence 1: confirm the topic (echo the query)
- Sentence 2–3: the direct answer in 40–60 words (featured snippet target)
- Sentence 4–5: what the reader will gain by reading further
- NO brand history. NO "In this article we will cover..."

[H2: First PAA question as exact heading]
- 2 sentences: direct answer to the H2 question
- Supporting detail: 150–300 words
- 1 internal link to a related page

[H2: Second PAA question]
- Same structure

[Continue for all PAA questions / semantic field clusters]

[H2: Original Insight Section]
- This is what separates this content from all competitors
- Personal experience, original data, case study, or unique perspective
- This is the E-E-A-T signal. Do not skip it.

[H2: FAQ — 5–8 questions]
- Each question: 1–3 sentence answer
- Add FAQPage schema
- These questions come from the remaining PAA/related searches not covered above

[Conclusion — 100–150 words]
- Summarize the key takeaway in 2 sentences
- Call to action: what should the reader do now?
- 1 internal link to a related resource
```

### Commercial / Comparison Content
```
Structure:
[Title] — "Best [X] for [Audience]: [Year] Guide"
[Meta] — include the comparison angle, include CTA

[Introduction — 100 words]
- Who this is for, what they'll find, our recommendation upfront

[Quick Comparison Table]
- HTML table: Product | Price | Best For | Rating
- Google loves pulling these into snippets

[H2: Our Top Pick — [Product Name]]
- First-hand experience signals: "We tested..." / "After X hours..."
- Pros, cons, who it's best for

[H2: Best for [Specific Use Case] — [Product Name]]
- Continue for each category

[H2: How We Tested / Our Methodology]
- This is the E-E-A-T anchor. Shows Google real expertise.

[H2: What to Look for in [Product Category]]
- Educational content that serves informational intent too

[FAQ — 5 questions]
```

### Transactional Content (Product/Service Pages)
```
Structure:
[Title] — product name + primary keyword + location if relevant
[Meta] — benefit + call to action

[Hero section — 50 words]
- Primary value proposition
- Trust signals: reviews count, years in business, guarantee

[Core offer — clear, structured]
- What it is, what it does, who it's for

[Social proof section]
- Real reviews, testimonials, case studies
- Review schema markup

[FAQ — 5 questions addressing purchase objections]
- FAQPage schema

[CTA — multiple placements]
```

## E-E-A-T Signal Checklist (run on every piece)

- [ ] Author name visible on page
- [ ] Author bio links to author page
- [ ] Author page has verifiable credentials
- [ ] Content includes first-hand experience language ("I/we tested/found/experienced")
- [ ] External links to authoritative sources (at least 2–3 per article)
- [ ] Claims are supported with sources or reasoning
- [ ] "Last updated" date is visible and accurate
- [ ] If YMYL: medical/legal/financial credentials are prominently displayed

## Freshness Protocol

Every piece of content has a decay rate:
- News/trending content: decay within 30 days — update or noindex
- Product comparisons: decay within 6 months — update pricing, add new products
- How-to guides: decay within 12 months — verify steps still work, update screenshots
- Fundamental explainers: decay within 24 months — verify facts, update statistics

Set a content calendar:
- Monthly: review posts ranking positions 8–20 — these are update candidates
- Quarterly: update all product comparison pages
- Annually: full content audit — remove or consolidate thin/underperforming pages

---

## The Originality Test

Before publishing any piece, ask:
1. Does this contain information that cannot be found by reading the top 5 results?
2. Does this contain a first-hand perspective that only we can provide?
3. Does this synthesize multiple sources into a new insight?

If all 3 are No — do not publish. Rewrite until at least 1 is Yes.
Google's Helpful Content system is built to identify content that exists
only to rank, not to genuinely help. That content gets sitewide penalties.
