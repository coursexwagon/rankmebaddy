# Module 2 — Entity Architecture Reference

## Entity Registration Sequence (execute in this order)

### Week 1 — Core entity establishment
1. Google Business Profile — create and fully complete (100% profile completion)
   - Business name: exact brand name, no keyword stuffing
   - Category: primary category must be precise
   - Description: 750 characters, include primary keyword naturally
   - Photos: minimum 10 photos uploaded at launch
   - Hours, phone, website: all consistent with domain

2. LinkedIn Company Page
   - Company name: exact brand name
   - About section: include brand description + primary niche keywords
   - Website: link to homepage
   - Industry: choose most precise option

3. Twitter/X Profile
   - Username: brand name or closest available
   - Bio: 160 chars, include niche keyword
   - Website: homepage link
   - Pinned tweet: most authoritative piece of content

4. YouTube Channel
   - Name: exact brand name
   - About section: full brand description + keywords
   - Channel art and logo: consistent with site branding
   - Link to website in channel settings

5. Crunchbase (for companies/startups)
6. Wikipedia (if notability criteria can be met — do not create unless it will survive AfD)
7. Wikidata entry (lower bar than Wikipedia, still creates Knowledge Graph signals)

### Week 2 — Author entity establishment
For every content creator publishing on the site:

1. Author page on the site:
   - URL: /author/firstname-lastname/
   - Full name, professional photo
   - Credentials relevant to the niche
   - Links to: LinkedIn, Twitter, published work elsewhere
   - Person schema markup

2. LinkedIn profile (personal, not company)
   - Current position: [Role] at [Brand Name]
   - Link to author page on site

3. Guest posts or bylines on other sites in the niche
   - Even 2–3 bylines on relevant sites creates author entity signals

### Knowledge Panel Acceleration
Google generates Knowledge Panels when it has sufficient entity signals.
Timeline: 8–16 weeks after consistent entity signals are established.

Signals that accelerate panel generation:
- Wikidata entry (strongest accelerator)
- Mentions in other Knowledge Graph entities' descriptions
- News coverage (even minor industry press)
- Wikipedia page (if achievable)
- Consistent social profiles with large followings

Once a panel appears, claim it via Google Search (click "Claim this knowledge panel")
and verify via one of the available verification methods.

# Module 5 — Behavioral Engineering Reference

## CTR Formula Library

### Title tag patterns by intent

**Informational:**
- "How to [Achieve Goal] (Even If [Common Obstacle])"
- "[Number] [Things/Ways/Tools] to [Achieve Goal] in [Year]"
- "The Complete Guide to [Topic]: [Year] Edition"
- "What Is [Topic]? [Benefit of Understanding It]"

**Commercial:**
- "Best [Product Category] for [Specific Use Case]: [Year] Tested"
- "[Number] [Product Category] Reviewed: Our [Year] Picks"
- "[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which Is Better for [Use Case]?"

**Transactional:**
- "[Service/Product] — [Primary Benefit] | [Brand Name]"
- "Get [Result] with [Product/Service] | [Trust Signal]"

### Power words that increase CTR
- Instant, Proven, Guaranteed, Free, New, Secret, Ultimate, Complete, Exact, Specific
- Use sparingly — overuse trains users to ignore them

### Meta description patterns
- "[Answer/Benefit]. Learn [Secondary Benefit] + [Additional Value]. [CTA]."
- "Discover [Number] [Things] that [Benefit]. Includes [Feature]. [CTA]."

## Pogo-stick Prevention Protocol

The opening 100 words are the most critical on any page.

**The 4-part opening structure:**
1. Acknowledgment (10 words): Echo the query language — confirm they're in the right place
2. Direct answer (40–60 words): The featured snippet paragraph — answer immediately
3. Credibility signal (20 words): Why this source has the answer
4. Promise (20 words): What they'll get by reading further

**Never open with:**
- "Welcome to our guide on..."
- "In this article, we will..."
- Brand history or background
- A question that restates the query without answering it

## Dwell Time Engineering Checklist
- [ ] Page has at least 1 image in the first scroll
- [ ] H2 subheading appears within first 300 words
- [ ] At least 1 internal link in the first 300 words
- [ ] At least 1 data point or statistic in the first 300 words
- [ ] A "curiosity gap" element appears between 30–40% of the page
- [ ] Video embedded (if relevant) — increases dwell by 2–3 minutes average
- [ ] FAQ section at the bottom extends session time
- [ ] "Related articles" section after conclusion — creates next-click behavior

# Module 6 — Authority Acquisition Reference

## Anchor Text Distribution Model

**Natural backlink profile distribution:**
- Branded anchors (brand name): 30–40%
- Naked URL anchors (https://domain.com): 15–20%
- Generic anchors (click here, read more, this article): 10–15%
- Partial match anchors (contains keyword but not exact): 15–20%
- Exact match anchors (exact target keyword): 5–10% maximum

**Warning:** If exact match anchors exceed 15%, SpamBrain risk increases significantly.
Audit anchor text distribution in Ahrefs or SEMrush monthly.

## Link Prospecting Templates

### HARO / Connectively (journalist outreach)
- Check daily for queries in your niche
- Respond within 4 hours — journalists work fast
- Response format:
  - 1 sentence: who you are and why you're qualified
  - 3–5 sentences: the specific answer to their question
  - 1 sentence: offer to provide more detail
- Do not pitch. Do not promote. Just answer the question.

### Guest Post Outreach Email
```
Subject: [Specific Article Idea] for [Their Site Name]

Hi [Name],

I've been reading [Their Site] for a while — specifically your recent piece on [specific article].

I'd love to contribute a guest post: "[Proposed Title]"

Quick overview:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

My background: [1 sentence credential relevant to the topic]

Would this work for [Their Site]?

[Name]
```

### Broken Link Building Process
1. Find a high-authority page in your niche with a broken outbound link
   (Ahrefs Site Explorer → Best by links → Filter for 404s)
2. Create content that replaces the broken resource
3. Email the site owner:
```
Subject: Broken link on [their page title]

Hi [Name],

Quick heads up — there's a broken link on [specific page URL].

The link to [broken URL] returns a 404.

I actually have a resource that covers the same topic: [your URL]

Might be a useful replacement if you're updating the page.

[Name]
```

## Link Velocity Rules
| Month | New Linking Domains (target) |

| 1 | 5–10 |
| 2 | 8–15 |
| 3 | 10–20 |
| 4–6 | 15–40/month |

# Module 7 — SERP Feature Capture Reference

## Featured Snippet Optimization by Format

### Paragraph snippets (most common — definitions, explanations)
- First sentence of the answer section: "[Term] is [definition]"
- 40–60 words maximum for the snippet paragraph
- Must be under the exact H2 question that matches the query
- Do NOT use bullet points for paragraph snippets — use prose

### List snippets (how-tos, steps, rankings)
- Use `<ol>` for ordered steps, `<ul>` for unordered lists
- Each list item: 1 clear sentence, 8–12 words ideal
- 5–8 items is the sweet spot
- The H2 above the list must match the query

### Table snippets (comparisons, data)
- Clean HTML table with clear headers in `<th>` tags
- First column: the items being compared
- Subsequent columns: attributes
- Maximum 6 columns for readability
- Include the query keyword in the table caption

## AI Overview Citation Strategy

AI Overviews pull from pages that:
1. Already rank in the top 10 for the query (prerequisite)
2. Have structured data / schema markup
3. Make clear, direct, factual statements (not hedged opinions)
4. Are from recognized entities in the Knowledge Graph
5. Are regularly updated (freshness signal)

**Content formatting for AI Overview citation:**
- Use clear declarative sentences: "X is Y" not "X could potentially be considered Y"
- Use numbered lists for processes — AI Overviews love to pull numbered steps
- Include statistics with sources — cited statistics get pulled into AI Overviews frequently
- Structured data is the direct line to AI Overview inclusion

**Monitoring AI Overview inclusion:**
- Search your target queries regularly and check if your site is cited
- Track impressions in Google Search Console — AI Overview citations show as impressions without clicks
- If cited: ensure the cited content is accurate and up to date
- If dropped from citation: check if content was recently updated and accuracy may have been flagged

# Module 8 — Compounding Loop Reference

## Google Search Console Workflow (Weekly)

### The GSC ranking gap report
1. Open GSC → Performance → Search Results
2. Filter: Position > 7 AND Position < 25
3. Sort by Impressions (descending)
4. Top 20 results = your update queue

These pages are:
- Indexed and understood by Google
- Receiving query impressions (Google considers them relevant)
- But not yet trusted enough to rank in top 7
- A content depth increase of 20–30% typically moves them 3–7 positions

### The CTR gap report
1. Open GSC → Performance → Search Results
2. Compare CTR to position benchmark:
   - Position 1: expected CTR ~30%+
   - Position 2: ~15%
   - Position 3: ~10%
   - Position 4–7: ~5–8%
3. Pages with below-benchmark CTR = title/meta description needs rewriting
4. These are high-leverage fixes — no new content needed, just metadata

### Content refresh protocol
When updating existing content:
1. Add new H2 sections covering PAA questions not in the original
2. Update all statistics and data to current year
3. Add a "Last updated: [Month Year]" timestamp (visible on page)
4. Add at least 1 new internal link to a more recent piece of content
5. Submit URL for re-indexing via GSC URL Inspection tool

# Module 9 — Multi-Site Scaling Reference

## The Content Network Architecture

When building multiple sites in the same topical cluster:

**Site 1 — The Authority Hub**
Broad coverage of the main topic. High DR target. Strong entity signals.
This is the flagship — invest most heavily here first.

**Site 2 — The Depth Specialist**
Goes 10x deeper into one subtopic of Site 1.
Example: Site 1 covers "personal finance" → Site 2 covers only "index fund investing"
Site 2 can rank for highly specific long-tail terms Site 1 won't target.
Site 1 links to Site 2 as the expert resource on that subtopic.

**Site 3 — The Geographic Variant**
Same topic, different country/language.
Uses the content system from Sites 1–2 but localized.
Example: Sites 1–2 target US → Site 3 targets South Africa / UK / Australia

### Cross-site linking rules
- Link between sites ONLY when topically relevant and contextually natural
- Never link between sites on every page — this looks like a link network
- 1–3 contextual editorial links between sites per month maximum per site pair
- The linking site must be the relevant authority on the linked topic

# Module 10 — Endgame Protocol Reference

## Knowledge Graph Entry Pathway

### Step 1 — Entity disambiguation
Google must be able to clearly distinguish your brand/author from other entities with similar names.
- Use full brand name consistently (never abbreviate differently across platforms)
- Disambiguate in descriptions: "[Brand] is a [niche] company based in [location]"
- The more unique the name, the faster entity recognition occurs

### Step 2 — Entity signal saturation
Google needs to see the entity mentioned consistently across trusted sources:
- Industry publications
- News sites (even small industry press)
- Partner/client sites
- Podcast appearances
- Social platforms
- Wikidata / Wikipedia

### Step 3 — Knowledge Graph connection
Your entity needs connections to entities already in the Knowledge Graph:
- Be mentioned in articles about known entities in your niche
- Quote and cite known experts (they may link back — creating bidirectional entity connection)
- Partner with established brands who already have Knowledge Panels

### Step 4 — Panel appearance and maintenance
Once a panel appears:
- Claim it immediately
- Update any inaccurate information
- Add knowledge panel "Suggest an edit" for any missing details
- Monitor monthly — panels can lose information if entity signals weaken

## AI Overview Citation Maintenance

Once your content is regularly cited in AI Overviews:
- Do not significantly change the structure of cited pages
- Keep cited statistics updated — outdated statistics lose citation status
- Monitor the specific phrasing Google uses in AI Overviews — optimize the source sentence to be more citable
- Add new sections to cited pages rather than rewriting existing cited sections

## The Endgame Metric Dashboard

Track these weekly once in endgame phase:
1. Branded search volume (Google Search Console — "Queries" filter for brand name)
2. Direct traffic percentage (GA4 — should increase as brand grows)
3. AI Overview citation count (manual SERP monitoring for target queries)
4. Knowledge Panel status (search brand name directly)
5. Domain Rating trend (Ahrefs — should compound upward without active link building)
6. Return visitor percentage (GA4 — high return rate = genuine authority signal)

When all 6 metrics are growing simultaneously, the compounding loop is fully operational.
The site no longer requires active optimization — it requires maintenance and continued value creation.

This is endgame. This is sovereign rank.
